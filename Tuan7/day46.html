Từ / Cụm từ Phiên âm Nghĩa Ví dụ
consent /kənˈsent/ (n, v) (sự) cho phép, đồng ý, bằng lòng, tán thành, chấp thuận
(permission or agreement)		 (n)
  • They can't publish your name without your consent.
  • The chairman was elected by common consent (=with most people agreeing).
  • divorce by mutual consent (=by agreement between both the people involved)
  • She was chosen as leader by common consent.

    • (v)
  • My aunt never married because her father wouldn't consent to her marriage.
  • She finally consented to answer our questions.
    		•
    agreement /əˈɡriː.mənt/ (n)
    1. sự đồng ý
    2. sự tán thành     (when people have the same opinion as each other)
    3. hợp đồng, giao kèo    		 1.
  • You'll have to get your parents' agreement if you want to go on the trip.

    • 2.
  • We're in agreement.
  • We're all in agreement.
  • I am in agreement with most of what he says.
  • Any changes to the plan require the agreement of everyone involved.
  • (n) disagreement: bất đồng
  • We’ve had a few disagreements, but we’re still good friends.
  • They have had several disagreements with their neighbours.

    • 3.
  • Their agreement expires next year.
  • They wanted to sign the agreement first.
  • By signing an agreement with the lawyer, the client agreed to pay two thousand dollars for his services.
  • (v) agree: đồng ý, tán thành
    - say yes
    - to have the same opinion
    • agree - agreed - agreed
  • "You're absolutely right," agreed Jake.
  • They agreed not to tell anyone about what had happened.
  • The bank has agreed (= is willing) to lend me £5,000.
    		•
    argument /ˈɑːrɡjə.mənt/ (n) sự tranh cãi, cãi nhau
    (syn) disagreement
  • The children had an argument about what game to play.
  • I had an argument with my boss.
  • She had a big argument with her husband.
  • There have been a lot of arguments about who was responsible for the accident.
  • I could hear my parents having an argument downstairs.
  • Phil got into an argument with a guy at the bar.
  • I'm sorry I'm not in a good mood. I had an argument with my husband this morning.
  • (v) argue: cãi nhau
  • Kids, will you stop arguing with each other?
  • We could hear the neighbours arguing.
    		•
    contract (n): /ˈkɑːn.trækt/
    (v): /kənˈtrækt/    		 (n) hợp đồng, giao kèo
    (v)
    1. kí hợp đồng (to sign a contract in which you agree formally that you will do something or someone will do something for you)
    2. dính, bị (bệnh) (to an illness)    		 (n)
  • a contract of employment
  • The contract between the two companies will expire at the end of the year.
  • Tyler has agreed a seven-year contract with a Hollywood studio.
  • The company had a contract to build a new hotel there.

    • (v1)
  • They are contracted to work 35 hours a week.
  • Our company was contracted to build shelters for the homeless.

    • (v2)
  • He contracted malaria while he was travelling.
  • My father contracted pneumonia last month.
