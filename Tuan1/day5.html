automobile /ˈɔːt̬ə.mə.biːl/ (n) ô tô
  • He's an automobile mechanic.
  • Our automobile industry is expanding.
  • Tom was killed in an automobile accident.
  • He got killed in an automobile accident.
  • An automobile ran over him.

    gratitude /ˈɡræ.t̬ə.tuːd/ (n) lòng biết ơn
  • The letter express gratitude for a contribution
  • I am full of gratitude to you for helping me.
  • She sent them a present to show/express her gratitude.
  • (adj) grateful:
         1. biết ơn
         2. dễ chịu (bóng mát - a grateful shade)

  • Phân biệt grateful và thankful
    • + grateful: biết ơn, cảm kích ai đó tốt với mình, đối xử tử tế với mình, có ân huệ với mình
    + thankful: thường dùng để giải tỏa, cảm thấy nhẹ nhõm khi những điều tồi tệ hoặc nguy hiểm đã không xảy ra
    commemorative /kəˈme.mə.reɪ.t̬ɪv/ (adj) để kỷ niệm
  • Everyone who takes part in the race will get a commemorative medal.
  • A commemorative plaque on the bridge records the name of the engineer.
    		•
    domestic /dəˈmes.tɪk/ (adj)
  • trong nước
  • trong nhà
    		•
  • Domestic flights and flights between the UK and Ireland do not required confirmation.
  • I'd rather not do domestic work.
  • I prefer to buy domestic rather than foreign products.
  • domestic violence: bạo lực gia đình
  • domestic chores: việc vặt trong nhà
    		•
    correspond /ˌkɔːrəˈspɑːnd/ (v) 1. tương đương, tương ứng, tương xứng
    2. trao đổi thư từ
  • correspond to something / correspond with something: tương ứng với
  • The American FBI corresponds to the British MI5.
  • The written record of the conversation doesn't correspond to (= is different from) what was actually said.
  • Her reply corresponded with yours, so I suppose I must believe you.
  • Your account of events does not correspond with hers.
  • the two windows do not correspond
    		•
    equivalent /ɪˈkwɪ.və.lənt/ (adj) tương đương
  • She's doing the equivalent job in the new company but for more money.
  • Eight kilometres is roughly equivalent to five miles.
  • Is $50 equivalent to about £30?
  • The two words are equivalent in meaning.
    		•
    predict /prɪˈdɪkt/ (v) dự đoán, đoán trước, nói trước
  • Nobody could predict the outcome.
  • The best way to predict your future is to create it.
  • Earthquakes are extremely difficult to predict.
  • Why can't scientists accurately predict the weather?
  • (n) prediction:
    1. sự nói trước, sự đoán trước
    2. lời dự đoán, lời tiên tri
    		•
    adverse /ˈæd.vɜːrs/ (adj) bất lợi, xấu
  • Lack of money will have an adverse effect on our research programme.
  • Some researchers believe that multi-tasking adversely affects workers' concentration and productivity.
  • The match has been cancelled because of adverse weather conditions.
  • The program teaches people about the adverse effects of alcohol and how to break the addiction.
  • (n) adversity: vận đen, vận rủi, tai họa, tai ương, nghịch cảnh
  • (n) adversary: kẻ thù, kẻ địch, đối thủ (enemy, opponent)
  • Fire proves gold, adversity proves men.
  • Their friendship was forged by sharing adversity.
  • Sometimes I think my math teacher is my adversary who loves to watch me fail.
  • Do you know who your adversary will be in the wrestling match?
